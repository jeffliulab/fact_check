1. The University of California (UC) is a public land-grant research university system in the U.S. state of California.
2. UC is headquartered in Oakland.
3. The UC system is composed of 10 campuses, along with numerous research centers and academic centers abroad.
4. UC was one of the founders of the Association of American Universities in 1900.
5. Since the 1970s, seven UC campuses, in addition to Berkeley, have been admitted to the Association of American Universities.
6. Berkeley, Davis, Irvine, Los Angeles, Santa Barbara, Santa Cruz, Riverside, and San Diego are considered Public Ivies.
7. UC faculty and researchers have won 75 Nobel Prizes as of 2025.
8. The UC system has a combined student body of 299,407 students, 26,100 faculty members, and 192,400 staff members.
9. UC's newest campus in Merced opened in fall 2005.
10. UC San Francisco enrolls only graduate and professional students in the medical and health sciences.
11. The University of California College of the Law located in San Francisco is legally affiliated with UC but is otherwise autonomous.
12. UC is governed by a Board of Regents whose autonomy from the rest of the state government is protected by the state constitution.
13. UC manages or co-manages three national laboratories for the U.S. Department of Energy: Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, and Los Alamos National Laboratory.
14. UC was founded on March 23, 1868.
15. UC initially operated in Oakland before moving to Berkeley in 1873.
16. UC affiliated itself with independent medical and law schools in San Francisco.
17. In March 1951, UC began to reorganize itself into a university system, with UC president Robert Gordon Sproul as the chief executive and Clark Kerr becoming Berkeley's first chancellor.
18. It was not until Kerr succeeded Sproul as UC president that UC was able to evolve into a university system from 1957 to 1960.
19. The Organic Act, establishing the University of California, was signed into state law by Governor Henry H. Haight on March 23, 1868.
20. The Hastings College of the Law, affiliated with UC in 1878, remains a UC affiliate and maintains its own board of directors.
21. The Master Plan for Higher Education, enacted into state law in 1960, provides that UC "shall be the primary state-supported academic agency for research".
22. The California State Normal School opened a second school in Los Angeles in 1882 to train teachers for Southern California.
23. In 1919, the state legislature transferred the Los Angeles State Normal School to UC control and renamed it the Southern Branch of the University of California.
24. In 1927, the Southern Branch became the University of California at Los Angeles.
