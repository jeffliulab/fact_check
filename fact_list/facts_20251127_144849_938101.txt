1. ISO 3166-1 is a standard defining codes for the names of countries, dependent territories, and special areas of geographical interest.
2. ISO 3166-1 defines three sets of country codes: ISO 3166-1 alpha-2, ISO 3166-1 alpha-3, and ISO 3166-1 numeric.
3. The alphabetic country codes were first included in ISO 3166 in 1974, and the numeric country codes were first included in 1981.
4. The country codes have been published as ISO 3166-1 since 1997, when ISO 3166 was expanded into three parts.
5. ISO 3166-1 is implemented in other standards and used by international organizations.
6. Codes for 249 countries, territories, or areas of geographical interest are assigned in ISO 3166-1.
7. The only way to enter a new country name into ISO 3166-1 is to have it registered in the United Nations Terminology Bulletin Country Names or the Country and Region Codes for Statistical Use of the United Nations Statistics Division.
8. The ISO 3166/MA may reserve code elements for other entities that do not qualify for inclusion based on the above criteria.
9. The ISO 3166/MA has reserved the two-letter combination EU for the purpose of identifying the European Union within the framework of ISO 3166-1.
10. The country names used in ISO 3166-1 are taken from the two UN sources, some of which are disputed.
11. The codes are chosen to reflect the significant, unique component of the country name in order to allow a visual association between country name and country code.
